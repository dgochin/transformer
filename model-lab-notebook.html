Entry 01 · 29 June 2026

The model writes fluently about nothing, and I can measure why

Every sentence it produces is grammatical. Whole paragraphs hold together. Then it falls into a loop, repeating clothing or repeating tense dialogue, and never escapes. The loop is not a glitch. It is the clearest sign of what the model has and has not learned.

Finding A

Longer context raised the ceiling, it did not remove it

Going from 256 to 512 context did what it was meant to do. The model now holds a scene for five or six sentences before it drifts, up from about two. A detective walks in, speaks in character, a door opens, a woman enters, and all of that stays consistent. That is a real, measured gain on the exact axis I was aiming at.

But the drift still arrives. The deeper limit is not how far back the model can see. It is how much of what it sees it can use. At 44M parameters it can read its own last 500 words but cannot treat them as binding facts. Seeing is not the same as enforcing consistency, and enforcement is what more parameters buy.

Finding B

It falls into register wells, and which one depends on the prompt

The model has learned the texture of each kind of writing extremely well: the clipped rhythm of a character description, the cadence of a tense standoff. But it has no underlying state, no model of who the person is or what the argument is about. So once it enters a register it runs out of real things to say almost at once, and circles that register’s most common phrases forever.

Describe a person and it spirals on clothing: a black suit, maybe a T-shirt, a blue tie, a grey shirt, never committing to one outfit because it is sampling the clothes sentence by sentence rather than remembering them. Put two people in a room and it spirals on dialogue: I’m not sure you’re going to do anything to me, repeated with small variations. Same disease, different room.

Finding C

Entropy turns the loop into a number I can watch

At every step the model gives a probability to all 36,000 tokens. Entropy summarises how spread out that is, in bits. The useful trick: two to the power of the entropy is roughly how many real choices the model has.

entropy = − Σ p · log₂(p) 0 bits = 1 forced choice  ·  1 bit = 2 choices  ·  2.6 bits ≈ 6 choices

The loop is not uniformly certain. It alternates. The spine of each repeated phrase collapses toward zero entropy (going→to→do→anything→to→me, each near one forced choice). Between repeats there are forks at 2 to 3 bits where the model truly has five or six options. The trap is that all five or six lead back into the same register. Real freedom, futile freedom: six doors, same room.

And it tightens as it runs. Each completed phrase enters the recent context, which makes the model even surer the same phrase comes next, so the spine entropy drops further every lap. The model digs its own groove deeper as it goes.

Finding D

Why it picks one well over another: the first few tokens decide

The model never chooses a topic for the whole passage. It only ever predicts the next token from the recent ones. So the well it lands in is set by what the last sentence happened to be, not by the prompt as a whole.

In the runs that spiralled on clothing, an early line introduced a person standing there, and in thriller prose a person appearing is followed by a description, so it fell toward clothing. In the run that spiralled on dialogue, the early tokens opened a quote before anyone got described, so it fell toward standoff filler instead. A small, semi-random early choice sends the ball down one slope. Same prompt, different seed, different well. The prompt nudges the starting position; the first half-dozen tokens pick the low point it rolls into.

Finding E · the root

Form without content, sharpened by a thriller-heavy corpus

This is the same gap I keep meeting, now visible as numbers. A human writing dialogue holds the unstated thing underneath: someone wants something, something is at stake, and every line moves it. The model holds the sound of tense dialogue with nothing underneath for the lines to be about, so the lines cannot advance anything. They resonate in place. The grammar is flawless; the text is about nothing; therefore it cannot progress.

The corpus makes it worse. Heavy on modern thrillers (ten Reacher books, plus DeMille, Cussler, Patterson), the data taught “describe a person” to mean “list clothing in clipped fragments” and “two people talk” to mean “trade tense one-liners” more strongly than a balanced corpus would. The model learned those grooves too well and over-applies them. The repetition penalty cannot fix it, because the penalty works on exact tokens while the loop is semantic: it just finds a near-synonym of the same hedge.

The well, triggered on purpose: the prompt ends mid-description, so the model drops straight in. The cloth% column is the whole story. Near 0% for three tokens, then it jumps to 40.9% at token 4 and stays pinned at 84–100% for the rest of the run. At tokens 57–58 (“black T-shirt”) entropy hits 0.00: the model has one forced choice, reciting rather than choosing. Across these 80 tokens the same detective wears a black suit, a coat, a white shirt, a uniform, a leather jacket, a T-shirt and a gray suit. Every sentence correct, no two agreeing, because there is no stored fact about the outfit to contradict.

Concepts I am keeping

entropy
The model’s own uncertainty about the next token, in bits. Two to the power of it is roughly the number of real choices. Same maths as the training loss, pointed at self-doubt rather than at surprise.
register well / attractor
A self-reinforcing loop the model falls into once it enters a kind of writing. There is one per register. The model rolls into whichever is nearest to where the last sentence left it.
loop spine vs fork
Spine: the forced, near-zero-entropy backbone of a repeated phrase. Fork: the open, high-entropy gap between repeats whose options all loop back in.
meaning-shaped hole
The model learned the form of language (register, rhythm, grammar) without a stable representation of what any of it is about. Fluent, and empty.
coherence-distance
How many sentences it holds a thread before drifting. 256 context bought about two. 512 context bought about five or six.

Next experiments

  • TinyStories should cure the loop. Its data is nothing but simple forward-moving plot, with no open-ended description or standoff register to fall into, and a small enough world that a small model can hold it. Prediction: the high-entropy forks start leading to different rooms. Test it by pointing the same entropy probe at a TinyStories model and checking whether the spine-collapse disappears.
  • Run the same prompt several times. Sampling is random, so each run takes a different early branch. If it lands in clothing on one run and dialogue on another, that confirms Finding D: the well is chosen by the first few tokens, not the prompt.
  • Mixed corpus, books plus a small TinyStories dose. Does easy, coherent narrative scaffold the hard novel prose, raising the floor on everything? Keep the dose small so the books still dominate.
